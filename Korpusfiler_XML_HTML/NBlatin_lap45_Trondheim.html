XV. TRONDHEIM

86.

Alexander III to Archbishop Eystein of Trondheim. Dispensation from the rules against consanguineous marriages for people living on an island twelve-days journey from Norway. Those who live in such isolated communities, in what most inhabitants are related, and who cannot travel abroad to seek marriage partners may be permitted to marry within the fifth, sixth, and seventh degrees.

87.

Alexander III to Archbishop Eystein of Trondheim. Permission to consecrate a biship of Oslo to succeed Thorstein, murdered by some of his subjects. The part of the diocese in which the murder took place should be deprived of episcopal presence forever; and the procuration which rightly belongs to the biship and the administration of the sacrament of confirmation («privilegium consignandi» should be transferred to other parts of the diocese at the bishop's will. The pope is amazed that the king of Norway has not avenged the crime.
WH 710; JL – (?) Benevento, December 1169
Alexander III Trudensi archiepiscopo. 24
Peruenit ad nos quod bone memorie quondam Asloensis 25 episcopus in quadam parte parochie sue a quibusdam perditionis filiis fuit crudeliter interemptus. Vnde licet tam piaculare flagitium grauissima sit animaduersione plectendum 26 , nos tamen postulatione tua inducti deuotioni tue auctoritate apostolica 27 concedimus ut in Asloensi 28 ecclesia episcopum ualeas ad honorem Dei et ecclesie ordinare, ita quidem ut illa pars episcopatus ubi tantum sacrilegium habuit exordium pontificali presentia perpetuo debeat carere, et diocesani episcopi procuratio 29 , que illum 30 de iure contingit, necnon et priuilegium 31 consignandi ad alias partes eiusdem diocesis pro ipsius episcopi arbitrio deferantur. Miramur autem, quod 32 tamen nec 33 sufficimus admirari, quod karissimus in Christo filius noster illustris Norweie rex tam horrendum scelus secundum collatam 34 sibi a Domino postestatem distulit uindicare 35 .

88.

Alexander III to Archbishop Eystein of Trondheim and his suffragans. Order that no cleric may devise to his relations or other secular heirs property which he obtained through his church. If any cleric of the province dies intestate, all such property should revert to the church in which he served. Property possessed by hereditary right may also be left to the church.
WH 49; JL – 1164-81 (? 1179-81)
Alexander III 36 Trundensi archiepiscopo et eius suffraganeis.
Ad aures nostras perlatum fuisse cognoscatis 37 quod quidam de clero uobis commisso non eam quam debent 38 circa salutem suam sollicitudinem et curam 39 adhibentes, ea que ipsis per ecclesias proueniunt 40 magis uolunt consanguineis 41 et heredibus aliis 42 suis secularibus relinquere quam eisdem ecclesiis de quarum bonis fuerant acquisita. Quidam etiam hec, etsi uellent, non possunt implere, quoniam legi uestre contraire noscuntur. Vnde quoniam ea que sanctorum patrum obuiant institutis alicuius consuetudinis obtentu non sunt 43 a Christiane professionis uiris aliquatinus obseruanda 44 , auctoritate apostolica statuimus ut, si aliquem clericum in uestris parochiis intestatum decedere forte contigerit, uniuersa eius bona que ipsi per ecclesiam prouenerunt, ad ecclesiam cuius clericus fuit nullius contradictione et appellatione obstante libere reuertantur. Si autem ea que iure hereditario possidet uoluerit ecclesie cuius clericus fuit relinquere, id efficendi liberam habeat facultatem.
Tradition: Cott. 4.73 (=C), Pet. 3.78 (=P).
Printed: W.H. in DA 2 (1938) 386-87 no. 3 (=C); in part Skånland in Historisk Tidsskrift 41 (1961-62) 138 (=P: 'Ad aures – statuimus' lin. 1-99).

89.

Alexander III to Archbishop Eystein of Trondheim and his suffragans. Response to miscellaneous questions (a) on the beginning and end of feastdays, and the observation of feasts with an octave; (b) on the canonical penance for homocide and it modifications; (c) on days and times at which liturgical genuflection is not permitted, and on genuflection during ordinations; (d) on regional differences regarding the date of the feast of the Holy Trinity, which the Roman Church itself does not celebrate; (e) on the cases in which an alter has to be reconsecrated, without any need for reconsecrating the church in which it stands; (f) on the removal from all service at the alter of a priest who has killed a pagan; (g) on the restitution to office, after penance, of priests who have lived in socalled marriage with a concubine.
